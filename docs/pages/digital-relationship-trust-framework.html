Digital Relationship Trust Framework

Building Trust in Every Digital Interaction

A layered architecture for digital relationships — from ephemeral transactions to enduring partnerships — grounded in cryptographic identity, verifiable provenance, and human governance.

Trust Architecture

The Trust Stack

Every digital relationship rests on layered foundations. Click each layer to explore how technology, fidelity, and governance compose into trustworthy interactions.

01 Digital Interaction RELATIONSHIP +

Transactional vs Interactional

Interactions range from single-use transactions (buy, verify, attest) to ongoing relational engagements (collaborate, co-create, govern). The trust requirements differ fundamentally.

Utility · Shared Domain · Context · Activities

Relationships form around shared utility and domain context. The EWM (Engagement-Witness-Mutual) pattern captures how parties discover shared activities and build context over time.

02 Assurance & Confidence FIDELITY +

Trust as Level of Risk

Trust is not binary — it's a dynamic confidence score derived from provenance, history, and verifiable credentials. Each interaction adjusts the assurance level.

Provenance

Every claim traces back to its origin through KEL (Key Event Log) entries and VC (Verifiable Credentials), providing cryptographic proof of authenticity and chain of custody.

03 Technology INFRASTRUCTURE +

Authentication & Encryption

Foundation layer: AuthN, protocols, encryption, cryptographic primitives. No passwords — FIDO2 passkeys bound to KERI AIDs provide biometric-secured, phishing-resistant identity.

Privacy & Path History

Selective disclosure through ACDC credential chaining. Path history is captured in append-only key event logs, making every identity action auditable without compromising privacy.

04 Governance KERI · ACDC +

Key Event Infrastructure

KERI provides the root-of-trust: self-certifying identifiers with pre-rotation, witnessed key events (icp, rot, ixn, dip, drt), and delegated authority chains.

Verifiable Credentials

ACDC credentials anchor domain-specific attestations to the KERI trust layer. The TEL (Transaction Event Log) tracks credential issuance, revocation, and lifecycle state.

05 Policy Stacks RULES +

Technical & Financial Policies

Machine-readable rules governing protocol behaviour, transaction limits, settlement windows, and compliance boundaries. Enforced at the infrastructure layer.

Human & Agent Policies

Rules of conduct for human actors and AI agents. The Human Conductor model ensures humans set policy while agents operate within scoped, time-limited, KERI-delegated authority.
